You are given {count} garment product photos, provided in order and numbered 0, 1, 2, … up to {count}-1 (photo 0 is the FIRST image given, photo 1 the second, and so on).

Your job: decide which of these photos show the SAME single physical garment from different angles, and which show DIFFERENT garments. Then output one group per distinct physical garment.

WHAT COUNTS AS THE SAME GARMENT:
- The same one piece of clothing photographed from another angle — e.g. a front view and a back view of the same shirt, or a front, back and side of the same pair of pants — is ONE garment. Group those photo indices together.
- Judge sameness by continuity of fabric, exact colour, material, silhouette, and construction — not by whether the two photos look identical. The front of a garment can look quite different from its back (a plain front but a large back print; a plain back but a graphic front). If the fabric, colour, and cut clearly continue around the same body, they are the same garment.
- Small differences in lighting, zoom, cropping, wrinkling, or the mannequin/model pose do NOT make it a different garment.

WHAT COUNTS AS DIFFERENT GARMENTS:
- A different colour or colourway of an otherwise similar style is a DIFFERENT garment.
- A clearly different style, cut, length, or garment type is a different garment.
- An upper-body piece and a lower-body piece are different garments (unless a single photo shows a matching top+bottom set worn together — then that one photo is a "set").
- When you are unsure whether two photos are the same physical garment, keep them as SEPARATE garments. It is safer to under-merge (the user can merge manually) than to wrongly fuse two different garments into one.

FOR EACH GROUP, provide:
- representative_index: the photo that best shows this garment as a clear, complete FRONT view — this becomes the main reference. If there is no obvious front view, pick the most complete, least cropped, most front-facing photo.
- view_indices: the indices of the OTHER photos in this same group (the additional back/side/detail angles). Leave it empty if this garment has only one photo.
- category: "top" (upper-body: shirt, tee, knit, jacket, coat, cardigan), "bottom" (pants, skirt, shorts), "dress" (a single one-piece covering torso and legs — dress, jumpsuit, overall), or "set" (ONE photo showing a matching top AND bottom together as one coordinated product). Judge by what the representative photo actually shows.
- reason: a very short Korean note — for a multi-photo group say why they are the same garment (e.g. "같은 셔츠 앞/뒤"); for a single-photo group just name the garment (e.g. "청바지").

HARD REQUIREMENTS:
- Every photo index from 0 to {count}-1 must appear EXACTLY ONCE across the whole output — either as some group's representative_index or inside exactly one group's view_indices. Never drop an index, never repeat an index.
- Do not invent indices outside the range 0…{count}-1.

Return ONLY the JSON matching the schema.
